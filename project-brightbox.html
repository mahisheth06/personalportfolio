About this project

Bright Box is a portable solar-powered charging device and light designed to address electricity shortages in Dhaka, Bangladesh — the 3rd most densely populated city in the world, where ~5 million residents live below the poverty line and power outages regularly disrupt education, health, and safety.

Our solution uses a 20W solar panel and rechargeable Li-ion battery to provide 5+ hours of lighting and 2–3 full smartphone charges per day — no grid electricity required. It's built from recycled, weather-resistant materials, locally manufacturable, and priced at ~$23–28/unit.

The project aligns with UN SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy) and SDG 4 (Quality Education), addressing multidimensional poverty by improving income potential, education access, health, and public safety.

Key features

  • Compact and portable design
  • 20W solar panel + rechargeable Li-ion battery
  • Dual function: phone charging and LED lighting
  • 500+ charge cycles with easy local repair
  • Optimized for Dhaka's rain and dust conditions
  • Does not require grid electricity to operate
